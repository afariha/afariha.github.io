From Pioneer Valley to Puget Sound

Sun, Sun, Sun! Here It Comes!

Today was so sunny that it compensated for yesterday's weather. The temperature was in the low 40s, still pretty chilly, but absolutely no cloud in the sky. The journey was pretty smooth, easy drive, only one stop at McDonald's. I didn't need to even refuel today. I crossed two state borders: Pennsylvania and Ohio. The moment I entered Ohio, suddenly the speed limit became 70. It has been a while since I have seen any speed limit higher than 65. For some weird reason, the maximum speed limit posted in the entire new England area is 65, which I call unrealistic speed limits, as no one every obeys such a low speed limit. When I started yesterday, I was seeing most cars with MA license plates, then I started seeing fewer, and now absolutely no MA cars. It is a very clear reminder that I am actually far from MA. I was getting glimpses of Lake Erie, which is kind of like an ocean, while driving. There were many cars (including a lot of police cars) beyond boring trucks today. I once thought to stop by Niagara falls, and also Rock & Roll Hall of Fame at Cleveland, but then I didn't. I wasn't really feeling like to add one hour to the driving. Sylvania is a small city, in fact it is very close to the larger city, Toledo.

Today's airbnb was fantastic. It is located at an upscale neighborhood and a very large one. It even had an outside patio with a BBQ area. I loved the wreath at the entrance door! Today I ordered food from some local restaurant called Black Rock Bar & Grill via Uber Eats. This too worked out very well.

I instantly liked the bedroom, it was so roomy, clean, and white, and most importantly, had a big window. I spent the entire afternoon and evening in the nice, warm, lovely sunlight coming through this window. I even attended a zoom meeting while enjoying the lovely afternoon! Although I promised myself that I won't work during this trip, but in my defense, it was neither a hard promise, nor the meeting was real work.
